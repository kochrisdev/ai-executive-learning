# Common Issues & Fixes

*Quick reference for resolving technical problems during live sessions.*

---

## Account & Access Issues

### "I can't log into ChatGPT"
- Check if they're on the correct URL: chat.openai.com (not openai.com)
- Try incognito mode — browser cache can cause login loops
- Have a backup: use Claude while they sort access

### "ChatGPT is saying it's at capacity"
- This happens during peak hours — switch to Claude for the exercise
- Always demo in Claude as backup for this reason

### "I don't have a Plus subscription"
- Free tier limits GPT-4o usage — exercises will still work but may be slower
- If needed, facilitator can share their screen for the demo portion

---

## Make.com Issues

### "Gmail won't connect / OAuth error"
- Make sure they're logged into their Gmail account in the same browser
- Try a different browser (Chrome usually most compatible)
- Alternative trigger: use Google Forms → Sheets instead of Gmail

### "I don't have an OpenAI API key"
- Facilitator should have a shared key available for Make.com exercises
- Alternative: skip the AI enrichment step and show the concept with a static text transform

### "My scenario ran but nothing happened"
- Check that the Gmail trigger has at least one email matching the filter
- Make sure the scenario is turned ON (toggle in top right)
- Run history shows what happened — check for errors

---

## Runway / Luma Issues

### "Video generation is taking too long"
- 30–120 seconds is normal — move to another tool while waiting
- Reduce clip length to 5 seconds for faster generation

### "Free tier won't let me generate"
- Runway free tier has 125 credits — should be enough for 2–3 short clips
- Have a pre-rendered example ready to show if credits run out

---

## WiFi / Bandwidth Issues

### "Tools are slow or timing out"
- This usually means too many people hitting APIs simultaneously
- Stagger heavy tasks: don't have all participants generate images at the same time
- Have participants work at slightly offset times (odd seats go, then even seats)

### "Complete internet outage"
- Switch to offline materials: printed templates and group discussion exercises
- Use pre-saved screenshots and output examples to continue demos
- Focus on strategy content (Phase 5) which has no tool dependency

---

## Participant Issues

### "I'm lost — everyone else seems to understand"
- Pull them aside during a break
- Go back to a simpler task (ask ChatGPT to summarize a paragraph they paste in)
- Pair them with a more confident participant for the next exercise

### "This won't work in my industry / company"
- Acknowledge the legitimate concern
- Reframe: what's the simplest, lowest-risk version that could work?
- Connect them with a specific use case from their own pre-work

### "Our IT team won't let us use any of these tools"
- Acknowledge — this is a real blockers for many participants
- Redirect to: what would you propose to IT to get a pilot approved?
- Recommend Microsoft Copilot as the enterprise-safe starting point

### Dominant participant taking over discussions
- Redirect: "Great point — let's hear from someone else. [Name], what's your take?"
- Use the round-robin format for share-outs to ensure all voices are heard

---

## Energy Management

### Post-lunch energy dip (Day 1 PM, Day 2 PM)
- Open with a 3-minute high-impact demo (Runway video generation, Suno music)
- Move into hands-on exercise quickly — doing is more energizing than listening

### Skeptical participant disrupting group energy
- Engage their skepticism productively: "Let's test that — give me a use case where you think AI would fail"
- Use their test as a live demo — often converts skeptics when the output surprises them

### Group is ahead of schedule and engaged
- Go deeper: "Let's build a second automation" or "Let's try a more complex prompt"
- Use the buffer activities from the run sheet
