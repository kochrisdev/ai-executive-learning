# Deep Dive Run Sheet

*Hour-by-hour delivery guide. Times are approximate — adjust based on group energy and pace.*

---

## Day 1

### 08:30 — Facilitator Setup
- Log into all tools: ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Runway, Make.com, Fireflies
- Test screen share and confirm WiFi
- Set out printed templates: AI Opportunity Canvas, Prompt Library reference card

---

### 09:00 — Welcome and Cohort Introductions (30 min)
**Facilitator script opening:**
> "Welcome. Over the next 2.5 days, you won't just learn about AI — you'll use it. Every session produces something real you can deploy this week. Let's start with who's in the room."

**Introductions:** Name, role, one sentence on what they hope to leave with.

**Pre-work debrief (10 min):** Ask:
- "Who found the tools more capable than expected? Less capable?"
- "What question came up that you couldn't answer?"
- Note recurring themes — address them during the day.

---

### 09:30 — State of AI in 2026 (25 min)
- Briefly cover what's changed since participants last engaged with AI
- Show the model landscape: GPT-4o, Claude Sonnet/Opus, Gemini, Llama
- Avoid slides where possible — use live tools to demonstrate as you explain
- Close with: "The tools change every 6 months. The underlying skill — knowing how to direct AI — doesn't."

---

### 09:55 — Live Demo: Three Tools, One Task (25 min)
**Demo task:** Ask all three tools the same business question in real time.
- Show the prompt being typed live, not pre-loaded
- Compare outputs side-by-side
- Ask group: "What do you notice? Which would you use and why?"

---

### 10:20 — Break (10 min)

---

### 10:30 — Hands-On Exercise: Real Task Sprint (30 min)
- Each participant uses the RACE framework on their own real task
- Facilitator and co-facilitator circulate — spend 2–3 min with each participant
- Watch for: vague prompts (coach to add role + context), over-ambitious first tasks (simplify)

---

### 11:00 — Cohort Share-Out (30 min)
- 3–4 volunteers share their prompt and output on the main screen
- Group discusses: what worked, what surprised, how they'd improve it
- Facilitator highlights 2–3 patterns or techniques demonstrated

---

### 11:30 — Personal AI Routing Map (20 min)
- Individual exercise: participants complete their routing map
- Brief pair discussion: compare maps with the person next to them

---

### 11:50 — Phase 1 Close + Phase 2 Preview (10 min)
- Summarize Phase 1 key takeaways
- Brief preview of afternoon: "We're going into visual and creative AI — you'll leave with an actual image or video brief you can use."

---

### 12:00 — Lunch (60 min)

---

### 13:00 — Phase 2: Visual & Creative AI
*(Follow [Phase 2 README](../deep-dive/phase-2-visual-creative/README.md) agenda)*

Key facilitator notes:
- Energy typically dips after lunch — start with a high-impact demo (Runway or Suno) to re-engage
- The gallery share at end of Phase 2 (15:45) is high-energy — let it run a few minutes over if the group is engaged

---

### 16:00 — Day 1 Close (15 min)
- What did you produce today? (Go around the room quickly)
- What tool surprised you most?
- Preview Day 2: automation and custom agents
- Optional evening assignment: try one more tool from today on your own

---

## Day 2

### 09:00 — Day 2 Opening and Overnight Reflections (15 min)
- Anyone try a tool last night? What happened?
- Address any questions that came up

---

### 09:15 — Phase 3: Intelligent Automation
*(Follow [Phase 3 README](../deep-dive/phase-3-automation/README.md) agenda)*

Key facilitator notes:
- Make.com hands-on is the most technically demanding exercise — budget extra time
- Have a simpler fallback automation ready if participants struggle with OAuth/API key setup
- The Fireflies demo lands best when you use it to show the actual session transcript from Day 1

---

### 12:00 — Lunch (60 min)

---

### 13:00 — Phase 4: Custom AI Agents
*(Follow [Phase 4 README](../deep-dive/phase-4-custom-agents/README.md) agenda)*

Key facilitator notes:
- The custom GPT build is the most popular session — let participants build something they're genuinely excited about
- Agent showcase is a high point — encourage humor and creativity alongside business utility
- If participants finish early, challenge them to add a second knowledge file or expand the system prompt

---

### 16:30 — Day 2 Close (15 min)
- Each participant states: "My agent does [X] for [audience]"
- Preview Day 3: AI strategy, business case, and plan presentations
- Reminder: come prepared to present your 90-day plan for 5 minutes

---

## Day 3

### 09:00 — Day 3 Opening: Reflections and Questions (20 min)
- Open floor: "What's the one thing from Days 1–2 you've already mentally committed to using?"
- Address final questions before moving into strategy

---

### 09:20 — Phase 5: AI Strategy
*(Follow [Phase 5 README](../deep-dive/phase-5-ai-strategy/README.md) agenda)*

Key facilitator notes:
- The 90-day plan presentations are the culminating moment — create ceremony around them
- Keep time discipline during presentations (5 min hard limit) so everyone presents

---

### 12:00 — Program Close (30 min)
- Facilitator reflection: what this cohort produced
- Participant commitments (round robin: one thing you're doing this week)
- Resource reminder: all templates, guides, and materials in this repository
- Exchange contact info; accountability pairs confirmed

---

### 12:30 — End of Program

---

## Timing Buffers

If running ahead of schedule, use these buffer activities:
- **Prompt battle:** Facilitator gives a task; participants race to write the best prompt in 2 minutes, then compare outputs
- **AI tool show-and-tell:** Any participant demos a tool or technique they discovered
- **Q&A on a specific use case** from the group

If running behind schedule, compress:
- The "Overnight Reflections" opening can be shortened to 5 minutes
- The automation opportunity mapping exercise can be completed as homework
- The gallery shares can move to cohort channel instead of live presentation
