## 2. Data Classification Rules

Before submitting content to any AI tool, classify it:

| Classification | Examples | Approved Tools |
|---|---|---|
| **Public** | Industry news, general concepts, public company info | Any approved tool |
| **Internal** | Meeting notes (generic), general processes, draft communications | Paid tiers with training opt-out; check tool's data policy |
| **Confidential** | Customer data, financial forecasts, legal strategy, product roadmaps | Enterprise tools only (Microsoft Copilot, Google Workspace AI) |
| **Restricted** | Passwords, credentials, regulated health/financial data, PII | Never in any AI tool |

**When in doubt:** Remove identifying details before submitting. Use fictional names and anonymized figures.

---

## 3. Output Verification Requirements

AI outputs must be verified before use according to the stakes involved:

| Use Case | Verification Required |
|---|---|
| Internal brainstorming, drafts | Light review — quality check only |
| External communications (email, reports) | Verify any specific claims or statistics |
| Customer-facing materials | Full review + manager approval |
| Legal, financial, or compliance content | Human expert review required before use |
| Any cited statistics or figures | Verify against primary source before publishing |

**The rule:** Treat every AI output as a smart first draft from a junior analyst — review before distribution.

---

## 4. Human-in-the-Loop Requirements

AI may not be the sole basis for the following decisions:

- Hiring, promotion, or termination of any individual
- Customer credit, pricing, or eligibility decisions
- Financial commitments exceeding $[amount]
- Any legally binding communication or agreement
- Disciplinary actions or performance evaluations

A human with appropriate authority must review and approve before any of the above actions are taken.

---

## 5. Transparency

- Do not represent AI-generated content as entirely your own work when asked
- When sharing AI-assisted work externally, use your judgment about disclosure — when in doubt, err toward transparency
- Do not use AI to impersonate specific individuals or generate fake testimonials
