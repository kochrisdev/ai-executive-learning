# Lesson 5.2 — The Build / Buy / Partner Decision

**Time:** ~10 minutes

---

## The Three Options

Every AI investment decision ultimately resolves to one of three approaches:

| Approach | What It Means | Time to Value | Cost | Control |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Buy** | Use off-the-shelf SaaS AI tools | Days | Low–Medium subscription | Low |
| **Partner** | Integrate AI via your existing software vendors or system integrators | Weeks–Months | Medium | Medium |
| **Build** | Develop custom AI using model APIs and your own data | Months–Years | High | High |

---

## When to Buy (Most Executives, Most of the Time)

Buy when:
- The use case is general (writing, summarizing, research, image generation)
- Speed of deployment matters more than customization
- You do not have proprietary data that changes the answer
- The tool already exists and works well

**Examples:** ChatGPT for writing, Fireflies for meeting notes, Make.com for automation, Runway for video

---

## When to Partner

Partner when:
- You want AI inside tools your team already uses (Microsoft, Salesforce, HubSpot)
- You need the AI to access your company's data securely
- You want vendor accountability for compliance and uptime
- You need enterprise-grade data handling but not bespoke AI

**Examples:** Microsoft Copilot for M365, Salesforce Einstein, HubSpot AI, ServiceNow AI

---

## When to Build

Build when:
- You have a proprietary dataset that creates competitive differentiation
- No existing tool solves the problem with acceptable accuracy
- The use case is core to your product or service (not just internal productivity)
- You have the engineering resources to build and maintain it

**Warning:** Most organizations overestimate their readiness to build. Build requires:
- AI/ML engineering talent
- MLOps infrastructure
- Data pipelines and governance
- Ongoing maintenance and retraining

Start with Buy. Move to Build only when you have exhausted what SaaS can offer and have a clear business case.

---

## Questions to Ask Before Any AI Vendor Decision

1. Where does my data go — and who can access it?
2. What happens to my data if I cancel?
3. What is the model — is it yours, OpenAI's, or another third party's?
4. What are the SLAs and uptime commitments?
5. What compliance certifications do you hold? (SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR, HIPAA)
6. What is the total cost at our projected usage scale?
7. Who is your largest customer in our industry, and can I speak to them?
