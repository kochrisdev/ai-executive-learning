# Lesson 5.3 — ROI Framing for AI Investments

**Time:** ~15 minutes

---

## The Two Types of AI ROI

AI investments generate value in two distinct ways. Most organizations only measure one.

| ROI Type | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Efficiency ROI** | Doing existing work faster, cheaper, or with fewer people | 5 hours/week saved per executive; 50% reduction in first-draft time |
| **Revenue ROI** | Creating new value, new products, or new revenue streams | AI-powered feature that increases conversion; new AI service offering |

Most organizations should start with Efficiency ROI — it is easier to measure, faster to realize, and builds the organizational muscle needed for Revenue ROI.

---

## Calculating Efficiency ROI

Simple framework:

```
Hours saved per person per week × fully-loaded hourly rate × team size × 52 weeks
= Annual efficiency value
```

**Example:**
- 5 hours saved/week per executive
- 10 executives in program
- $200/hour fully-loaded cost
- 5 × 10 × 200 × 52 = **$520,000 annual value**

This is conservative — it does not include speed of decision-making, quality improvements, or competitive advantages from faster execution.

---

## The Total Cost of AI

Do not undercount costs:

| Cost Category | What to Include |
|---|---|
| **Tool subscriptions** | Per-seat or usage-based pricing for all tools |
| **Training and onboarding** | This program, ongoing upskilling |
| **IT integration and security** | Setup, SSO, data governance |
| **Management overhead** | Policy development, vendor management, incident response |
| **Productivity dip** | Short-term slowdown while people learn new tools |

---

## Common Business Case Mistakes

| Mistake | Reality |
|---|---|
| Projecting 100% adoption immediately | Realistic adoption takes 3–6 months to reach 80%+ |
| Counting hours saved as headcount reduction | Time savings translate to capacity, not always direct cost reduction |
| Ignoring the quality dimension | AI often improves output quality, which has value beyond speed |
| Measuring only the pilot | Scale assumptions must account for change management and training costs |

---

## The 30-Day Proof Point

Before building a full business case, run a 30-day measurement:
1. Identify 3 people using AI tools actively
2. Ask them to log time saved on specific tasks for 30 days
3. Calculate average savings × your organization's fully-loaded cost rate
4. Extrapolate to your full team
5. Present this as your evidence base for the investment case

Real data from your own organization is more persuasive to any board or CFO than any vendor case study.
