# Lesson 5.4 — Setting Your AI North Star

**Time:** ~10 minutes

---

## What Is an AI North Star?

Your AI North Star is a simple, specific statement of what success looks like for your function in the next 90 days. It is not a technology vision — it is a business outcome statement.

**Examples:**
- "Every member of my marketing team can produce campaign briefs and content drafts using AI, cutting our agency dependency by 30% within 90 days."
- "Our sales team uses AI-assisted call prep and follow-up drafting, increasing average deal velocity by 15% this quarter."
- "My finance function automates 3 recurring reporting workflows, freeing 10 hours per analyst per month."

Notice what these statements have in common:
- They name the team (not just "the company")
- They specify a behavior change (what people will do differently)
- They include a measurable outcome
- They have a time horizon

---

## Leading vs. Lagging Indicators

| Indicator Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Leading (track weekly)** | % of team using AI tools; number of prompts run; workflows automated |
| **Lagging (track monthly)** | Hours saved; output quality scores; revenue or cost impact |

Do not wait 90 days to discover whether adoption is happening. Track leading indicators weekly and intervene early if usage is not growing.

---

## Your Role as Executive Sponsor

The difference between AI programs that succeed and those that fade is executive sponsorship. Specifically:

- **Model the behavior:** Use AI visibly in meetings, share outputs, demonstrate the tools
- **Protect time:** Block time for your team to learn and experiment, especially early
- **Celebrate wins:** When someone saves 3 hours with AI, name it publicly
- **Remove blockers:** IT access, tool provisioning, policy approvals — these are your job to unblock
- **Set the expectation:** "AI fluency is a core skill for this team going forward" needs to come from you
