# Lesson 2.2 — ChatGPT: Your First 30 Minutes

**Time:** ~25 minutes

---

## Interface Walkthrough

When you open ChatGPT, you'll see:

- **Left sidebar:** Your conversation history, organized by date
- **Center:** The chat window
- **Bottom:** The input box where you type prompts
- **Top right:** Your account and settings

Key settings to configure first:
1. Click your name (bottom left) → **Customize ChatGPT**
2. Add context about yourself: your role, your industry, how you want responses formatted
3. This context is injected into every conversation automatically

---

## Your First 5 Prompts as an Executive

Try each of these — use generic/fictional context until you're comfortable with privacy settings:

### Prompt 1: Summarize
```
Summarize the following article in 5 bullet points written for a CEO audience.
Highlight any strategic or financial risks.

[Paste any article text here]
```

### Prompt 2: Draft
```
Write a 3-paragraph internal memo announcing a new hybrid work policy.
Tone: direct, supportive, and clear.
Key points: 3 days in office required, flexibility on which days,
effective from next quarter.
```

### Prompt 3: Compare
```
Create a comparison table of the pros and cons of expanding into
the European market vs. the Southeast Asian market for a mid-sized
B2B software company with $50M ARR.
```

### Prompt 4: Analyze
```
I'm reviewing a vendor proposal. What are the top 10 questions
I should ask before signing a $500K enterprise software contract?
```

### Prompt 5: Devil's Advocate
```
Here is a strategic initiative I'm considering:
[Describe your initiative in 2-3 sentences]

Play devil's advocate. What are the 5 strongest objections
a skeptical board member would raise?
```

## Understanding ChatGPT's Memory

ChatGPT can remember facts about you across conversations (if Memory is enabled):

- Go to Settings → Personalization → Memory → On
- Click "Manage Memories" to see what it has stored
- You can add memories manually: "Remember that I'm the CEO of a 200-person fintech company"

This means over time, ChatGPT becomes more useful as it accumulates context about your role and preferences.

## The GPT Store: Pre-Built Assistants

ChatGPT's GPT Store contains thousands of pre-built AI assistants:

- Click "Explore GPTs" in the left sidebar
- Useful for executives: **Consensus** (research papers), **Canva** (design), **Diagrams** (visual flowcharts), **Scholar AI** (academic research)
- In the Deep Dive, you will build your own custom GPT — this is the store where it would live

## Common Beginner Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Too-vague prompt ("Tell me about marketing") | Add role, context, format ("As a CMO in B2B SaaS, give me 5 marketing channels...") |
| Accepting first output as final | Always ask: "Make it shorter," "More formal," "Add a risks section" |
| Not providing context | Tell it your industry, company size, and specific situation |
| Starting a new chat for follow-ups | Continue in the same conversation — context accumulates |
