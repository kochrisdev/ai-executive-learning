## What Is a Large Language Model?

A Large Language Model (LLM) is software trained on vast amounts of text to predict what words, sentences, or ideas logically follow from a given input. It does not "think" — it pattern-matches at an extraordinarily sophisticated level.

When you type a question into ChatGPT or Claude, the model:
1. Breaks your text into tokens (word fragments)
2. Looks up patterns it learned during training
3. Generates a statistically likely, coherent response
4. Streams that response back to you word by word

This is not magic. It is very good autocomplete — but that simplification undersells how genuinely useful it is for business.

## AI vs. Machine Learning vs. Generative AI

| Term | What It Means | Executive-Level Analogy |
|---|---|---|
| **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** | Broad umbrella term for machines performing tasks that normally require human intelligence | The whole field of "smart software" |
| **Machine Learning (ML)** | AI that learns from data to make predictions or decisions | A system that learns your customer churn patterns and flags at-risk accounts |
| **Generative AI** | A subset of ML that *creates* new content — text, images, audio, video, code | A system that drafts your board memo, generates a product image, or writes code |

For this program, we focus on **Generative AI** — the tools you can use today without a data science team.

## Why 2024–2026 Is a Genuine Inflection Point

Previous AI hype cycles (2016 chatbots, 2019 "AI strategy" consulting decks) failed because:
- Models were narrow — good at one task, useless at others
- They required significant technical integration to deploy
- Results were inconsistent and unreliable in real business settings

What changed:
- **Foundation models** — a single model can write, reason, summarize, code, and converse
- **Accessible interfaces** — any executive can use it via a browser, no engineering required
- **Speed of capability growth** — GPT-3 (2020) to GPT-4o (2024) is a larger leap than the prior decade combined
- **Integration into existing tools** — AI is now inside Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Salesforce, and Slack

The threshold has crossed from "interesting experiment" to "competitive necessity."

## What AI Cannot Do — And Why This Matters to You

Understanding limitations is as important as understanding capabilities. AI tools:

| Limitation | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **Hallucinate** — confidently generate false information | Never rely on AI output without verification for high-stakes decisions |
| **Have a knowledge cutoff** — trained on data up to a certain date | May not know recent events, market moves, or regulatory changes |
| **Cannot access live systems** — unless specifically integrated | It cannot pull your actual CRM data or read your live P&L without a connector |
| **Do not "know" your business** — unless you tell them | Generic prompts yield generic answers; context is everything |
| **Cannot replace judgment** — they optimize for plausibility, not truth | The final decision is always yours |

A useful rule: treat every AI output as a **strong first draft from a very fast junior analyst** — smart, quick, useful, but unverified.

---

## Key Takeaway

AI is not sentient, not infallible, and not a threat to replace executive leadership. It is a powerful tool for accelerating knowledge work — and right now, the executives who learn to direct it well will have a significant advantage over those who do not.
