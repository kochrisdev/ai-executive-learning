# Lesson 1.2 — The AI Ecosystem Map

**Time:** ~20 minutes

---

## The Major Players

You do not need to track every AI company. You need to understand the five platforms that matter most for enterprise executive use.

| Company | Key Product | Strength | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OpenAI** | ChatGPT / GPT-4o | Breadth, speed, plugin ecosystem | General-purpose reasoning, writing, coding |
| **Anthropic** | Claude | Nuanced reasoning, long documents, safety | Analysis of complex materials, sensitive topics |
| **Google** | Gemini / NotebookLM | Integration with Google Workspace, search | Research, document summarization, Workspace users |
| **Microsoft** | Copilot (365) | Deep Office integration | Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Teams automation |
| **Meta** | Llama (open-source) | Customizable, self-hosted | Organizations that need to run AI on their own infrastructure |

### The Practical Rule
- For **personal productivity** → ChatGPT or Claude
- For **organization-wide deployment** → Microsoft Copilot or Google Workspace AI
- For **custom AI products** → OpenAI API, Anthropic API, or Llama

---

## Closed vs. Open-Source Models

| Type | Examples | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Closed / Proprietary** | GPT-4o, Claude, Gemini | Best performance, no setup, vendor-managed safety | Data goes to vendor servers, subscription cost, vendor lock-in |
| **Open-Source** | Llama 3, Mistral, Phi | Run on your own servers, full data control, customizable | Requires engineering resources, you own the maintenance |

**For most executives:** closed models are the right starting point. Open-source becomes relevant when your legal or security team has concerns about data leaving your infrastructure.

## AI Already Embedded in Your Tools

You may already have AI available without new subscriptions:

| Tool You Already Use | AI Capability Available |
|---|---|
| Microsoft 365 (with Copilot license) | Draft emails, summarize meetings, analyze spreadsheets |
| Google Workspace (Gemini) | Draft in Docs, summarize in Gmail, generate in Slides |
| Salesforce (Einstein AI) | Summarize opportunities, draft outreach, forecast |
| Slack (AI features) | Summarize channels, recap threads |
| LinkedIn (AI features) | Draft connection messages, job description writing |
| Zoom (AI companion) | Meeting summaries, action items |

**Action:** Check with your IT team which of these are already licensed and available in your organization.

---

## The SaaS AI vs. Custom AI Decision

As an executive, you will be asked to make or approve this decision:

| Approach | What It Means | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **SaaS AI Tools** | Use tools like ChatGPT, Claude, or Copilot as-is | 80% of executive use cases; fastest time to value |
| **AI-Integrated Platform** | Your existing software vendor adds AI to their product | When you want AI inside the tools your team already uses |
| **Custom AI Build** | Engineers build AI capabilities using model APIs | When you have unique data, unique workflows, or competitive differentiation at stake |

Start with SaaS. Build custom only when you have a clear business case and the technical resources to support it.

---

## Key Takeaway

The AI ecosystem is large but not complicated to navigate at the executive level. Know the five major players, understand closed vs. open, check what's already in your existing tools, and start with SaaS before committing to custom builds.
