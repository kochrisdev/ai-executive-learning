# Lesson 3.3 — Prompt Patterns for Common Executive Tasks

**Time:** ~25 minutes

---

## Ready-to-Use Prompt Library

Copy, adapt, and use these patterns immediately. Replace bracketed sections with your specifics.

---

### 1. Document Summarization
```
You are an executive assistant with expertise in [your industry].

Summarize the attached document for a [CEO / board / investor] audience.

Structure your summary as:
- 3-sentence overview
- 5 key points (bullet format)
- Top 3 risks or concerns flagged
- Recommended action or decision required

Keep the total under 400 words. Flag any claims that appear unsubstantiated.
```

---

### 2. Competitive Analysis
```
You are a competitive intelligence analyst with deep knowledge of [industry].

Compare [Company A] and [Company B] across the following dimensions:
- Business model and revenue streams
- Target customer segment
- Pricing strategy
- Key differentiators and weaknesses
- Recent strategic moves (past 12 months)

Present as a structured table. After the table, write a 2-paragraph
strategic assessment of what [my company's type] should do in response.
```

---

### 3. Board / Investor Communication
```
You are a seasoned CFO and board communicator.

Write a board update for our [Q3 / Q4 / monthly] review.
Context: [2-3 sentences about current company situation]

Structure:
1. Performance Highlights (3 bullets)
2. Key Challenges (2 bullets)
3. Decisions Required from the Board (numbered list)
4. Looking Ahead: Next 90 Days (3 bullets)

Tone: Confident, transparent, and forward-looking. Under 500 words.
```

---

### 4. Strategy Stress Test
```
You are a skeptical board member with a track record of identifying strategic blind spots.

I am about to present the following strategic initiative:
[Describe your initiative in 3-5 sentences]

Your job is to challenge this plan aggressively. Identify:
1. The 3 most likely ways this initiative fails
2. The assumptions that, if wrong, would be most damaging
3. Competitive responses we may have underestimated
4. Internal execution risks we may have glossed over

Be direct. Do not soften your critique.
```

---

### 5. Decision Support
```
I am making a decision between the following options:
Option A: [Describe]
Option B: [Describe]
Option C: [Describe if applicable]

Context: [Company situation, constraints, timeline]
Criteria that matter most: [e.g., speed, cost, reversibility, team capability]

Please:
1. Build a pros/cons analysis for each option
2. Identify the key trade-off I am making
3. Tell me what information I am probably missing
4. Give a recommendation with your reasoning
```

---

### 6. Meeting Preparation Brief
```
I have a [30 / 60]-minute meeting with [role/title] from [company].
The purpose of the meeting is: [brief description]

Please prepare:
1. A 5-point background brief on [company/person] (use public information only)
2. My top 3 objectives for this meeting
3. 5 insightful questions I should ask
4. 3 potential objections they may raise and how I should respond
5. Suggested next steps to propose at the end
```

---

### 7. Internal Announcement
```
You are a Chief People Officer with expertise in change communication.

Write an internal announcement for: [what is being announced]
Audience: [all staff / leadership team / specific department]
Context: [background on the change, timeline, who is affected]

Requirements:
- Acknowledge the human impact honestly
- Be clear about what changes and what does not
- Include available support or resources
- End with an invitation for questions or dialogue
- Tone: [empathetic / direct / celebratory — choose one]
- Length: under 350 words
```

---

### 8. Vendor / Partner Evaluation
```
I am evaluating [vendor/product name] for [use case].
Our company: [brief description, size, industry]
Key requirements: [list 4-5]
Budget range: [if applicable]

Please:
1. List the top 5 questions I should ask the vendor in our evaluation call
2. Identify common contract terms I should scrutinize
3. Suggest 3 competing alternatives I should consider
4. Flag any known risks or limitations of this vendor/category
```

---

## Building Your Own Library

As you use AI daily, save the prompts that work. A personal prompt library is one of the highest-leverage productivity tools an executive can build. Start a simple document or Notion page titled "Prompts That Work" and add to it weekly.
