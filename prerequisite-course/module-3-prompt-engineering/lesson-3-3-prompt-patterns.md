# Lesson 3.3 — Prompt Patterns for Common Executive Tasks

**Time:** ~25 minutes

---

## Ready-to-Use Prompt Library for Impact-Makers

Copy, adapt, and use these patterns immediately. Replace bracketed sections with your specifics. Examples are written for the kinds of work DreamLabs Fellows do — social enterprises, community initiatives, advocacy, and sustainable ventures in Myanmar.

---

### 1. Document Summarization
```
You are a research analyst with expertise in [your impact area — e.g., community
health, sustainable agriculture, education, youth development].

Summarize the attached document for a [donor / community leader / NGO partner /
young leader] audience.

Structure your summary as:
- 3-sentence overview
- 5 key points (bullet format)
- Top 3 risks or challenges flagged
- Recommended action or next step

Keep the total under 400 words. Flag any claims that appear unsubstantiated.
```

---

### 2. Grant Proposal Draft
```
You are an experienced grant writer for social impact organizations in Southeast Asia.

Draft a 500-word grant proposal section for [foundation/funder name].
Our initiative: [2-3 sentence description]
Target community: [who we serve, where in Myanmar]
The problem we solve: [specific challenge]
Our approach: [how we work]
Evidence of impact so far: [any results or proof points]

Requirements:
- Tone: compelling, specific, and community-centered
- Lead with the human story, not the organization
- Be concrete about what the funding will enable
- End with a vision statement tied to sustainable change
```

---

### 3. Impact Report
```
You are a communications specialist for a social enterprise in Myanmar.

Write an impact update for our [quarterly / annual] report.
Context: [2-3 sentences about our work this period]

Structure:
1. Headline Impact (1-2 sentences — most important number or story)
2. Key Achievements (3 bullets)
3. Challenges We Faced and How We Responded (2 bullets)
4. What's Next (3 bullets)
5. A quote from a community member or beneficiary (you may draft a representative quote)

Tone: Honest, warm, and evidence-based. Under 500 words.
```

---

### 4. Initiative Stress Test
```
You are a critical advisor with experience supporting social enterprises in Southeast Asia.

I am about to launch the following initiative:
[Describe your initiative in 3-5 sentences, including the community it serves]

Your job is to challenge this plan honestly. Identify:
1. The 3 most likely ways this initiative fails or falls short of impact
2. Assumptions about the community that may not hold
3. Operational or funding risks we may have underestimated
4. Unintended consequences we should consider

Be direct and constructive. I want to build something that actually works.
```

---

### 5. Decision Support
```
I am making a decision between the following options for my initiative:
Option A: [Describe]
Option B: [Describe]
Option C: [Describe if applicable]

Context: [Initiative situation, constraints, resources, timeline]
Criteria that matter most: [e.g., community impact, sustainability, cost, speed]

Please:
1. Build a pros/cons analysis for each option
2. Identify the key trade-off I am making
3. Tell me what information I am probably missing
4. Give a recommendation with your reasoning
```

---

### 6. Partner or Stakeholder Meeting Prep
```
I have a [30 / 60]-minute meeting with [role/title] from [organization / community].
The purpose of the meeting is: [brief description]

Please prepare:
1. A 5-point background brief on [organization / context]
2. My top 3 objectives for this meeting
3. 5 insightful questions I should ask
4. 3 potential concerns they may raise and how I should respond
5. Suggested next steps to propose at the end
```

---

### 7. Community Communication
```
You are a community engagement specialist with experience in Myanmar.

Write a community announcement for: [what is being communicated]
Audience: [community members / local leaders / youth / parents — choose]
Context: [background, timeline, who is affected]

Requirements:
- Use simple, clear language accessible to a broad audience
- Be honest about what is changing and what support is available
- Show respect for the community's role and wisdom
- End with an invitation for questions or dialogue
- Tone: [warm and respectful / direct / encouraging — choose one]
- Length: under 300 words
```

---

### 8. Pitch / Presentation Deck Outline
```
You are a pitch coach helping social entrepreneurs in Southeast Asia.

Create a 10-slide presentation outline for my initiative.
Initiative name: [name]
The problem: [what challenge in Myanmar we're addressing]
Our solution: [how we work]
Evidence: [what we've shown so far]
The ask: [what we need — funding, partners, volunteers, etc.]
Audience: [investors / donors / community / DreamLabs mentors / government]

For each slide: provide a title, 3 bullet points of content, and one visual suggestion.
Tone: compelling, grounded, and human.
```

---

### 9. Research Synthesis
```
You are a research analyst specializing in [education / health / environment /
agriculture / youth development — choose] in Southeast Asia.

I have conducted [interviews / surveys / desk research] on [topic].
Here are my raw notes: [paste notes or key findings]

Please:
1. Identify the 3-5 most important themes across all the data
2. Highlight any surprising or contradictory findings
3. Identify gaps — what questions remain unanswered?
4. Suggest 3 actions or decisions this research should inform
```

---

## Building Your Own Library

As you use AI daily, save the prompts that work. A personal prompt library is one of the highest-leverage tools an impact-maker can build — and it compounds over time. Start a simple document or Notion page titled "Prompts That Work" and add to it weekly.

In the Innovation Studio, we encourage Fellows to share their best prompts with the cohort. When one Fellow discovers a powerful pattern for grant writing, impact measurement, or community communication — the whole cohort benefits.
