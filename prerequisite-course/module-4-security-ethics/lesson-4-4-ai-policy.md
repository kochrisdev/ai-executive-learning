# Lesson 4.4 — Building a Basic AI Policy for Your Team

**Time:** ~10 minutes

---

## Why Your Team Needs a Policy Now

Without a policy, your team will:
- Use consumer AI tools with confidential data (already happening everywhere)
- Get inconsistent results with no shared prompt standards
- Avoid AI out of fear or use it recklessly out of enthusiasm
- Operate without accountability when something goes wrong

A one-page policy prevents all of this. It does not need to be a legal document — it needs to be clear, practical, and easy to follow.

---

## What a Lightweight Policy Covers

A practical team-level AI policy has five elements:

### 1. Approved Tools
List which AI tools are officially approved for use and at what tier:
- Example: "ChatGPT Plus and Claude Pro are approved for individual use. ChatGPT Enterprise is provisioned for teams handling customer data."

### 2. Data Classification Rules
What data can go into which tools:
- Example: "Public and internal-general data may be used in approved tools. Confidential and restricted data requires enterprise-tier tools with appropriate data agreements."

### 3. Output Verification Requirements
How outputs must be treated before use:
- Example: "All AI-generated content containing specific data (statistics, citations, financial figures) must be verified before distribution or publication."

### 4. Human-in-the-Loop Requirements
Which decisions require human review:
- Example: "No AI output may be used as the sole basis for hiring, termination, financial commitment over $[X], or customer-facing legal or compliance communications."

### 5. Reporting and Questions
Where to go with concerns:
- Example: "Questions about AI tool use or data handling should be directed to [name/team]. Incidents involving possible data exposure should be reported immediately to IT Security."

---

## Policy Anti-Patterns to Avoid

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| "Don't use AI tools" | Unenforceable; drives usage underground |
| 20-page legal document | No one reads it; complex policies create confusion |
| No update mechanism | AI landscape changes fast; policy becomes stale within months |
| No approved alternatives | Prohibition without provision creates shadow AI usage |

---

## Policy Update Cadence

Revisit your AI policy:
- When a major new tool is released or adopted by your team
- When a significant AI incident occurs (internally or publicly)
- At minimum every 6 months — the AI landscape changes fast

A good policy is a living document, not a filed-and-forgotten compliance artifact.
